How PCOS is Diagnosed?

Tests and evaluations used to identify PCOS

Diagnosis Steps

Doctors use a combination of physical examinations, blood tests and ultrasound imaging to confirm PCOS.

  • Blood Tests: Check hormone levels like LH, FSH, Testosterone
  • Ultrasound: Detects ovarian cysts and ovary size
  • Medical History: Menstrual cycle patterns, past symptoms
  • Physical Exam: Skin changes, weight, hair growth

Why is early diagnosis important?

Early detection helps prevent fertility issues, diabetes, weight problems, and long-term hormonal imbalance.
