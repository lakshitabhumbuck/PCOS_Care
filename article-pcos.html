What is PCOS?

A clear, simple explanation of Polycystic Ovary Syndrome

Understanding PCOS

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) is a hormonal disorder that affects women during their reproductive years. It occurs when the ovaries produce an abnormal amount of androgens — male hormones that are normally present in small amounts.

Key Features of PCOS

  • Irregular or missed periods
  • Excess male hormones (androgens)
  • Multiple small cysts in ovaries
  • Weight gain and metabolic issues
  • Fertility challenges

Is PCOS treatable?

PCOS is a manageable condition. With early detection and lifestyle modifications such as healthy diet, exercise, stress reduction and medical treatment, most symptoms can be controlled effectively.
